Publications

Invertible generative models for inverse problems: mitigating representation error and dataset bias.

In an imaging inverse problem, one must recover missing information about a target image using prior assumptions on the image structure. We show that Invertible Neural Networks can be used to vastly outperform classical approaches when one has access to a dataset of known images. Published in ICML 2020.

M. Asim*, M. Daniels*, O. Leong, P. Hand, and A. Ahmed. Invertible generative models for inverse problems: mitigating representation error and dataset bias. In Proceedings of Machine Learning and Systems 2020, pages 4577–4587. 2020. [Online]

Statistical Distances and Their Implications to GAN Training.

This is an interactive article about the role of statistical distances like Kullback Leibler Divergence and Earth Mover's Distance in training Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). Honorable mention for best submission.

M. Daniels. Statistical Distances and their Implications to GAN Training. In the VISxAI Workshop at IEEE VIS 2019. [Online]
