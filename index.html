Publications

Invertible generative models for inverse problems: mitigating representation error and dataset bias.

In an imaging inverse problem, the goal is to recover missing information about a target image by leveraging an assumed prior distribution from which the target was sampled. We show that Invertible Neural Networks, when trained as a generative model of a class of natural images, can provide a strong image prior in a variety of inverse problems. Published in ICML 2020.


M. Asim*, M. Daniels*, O. Leong, P. Hand, and A. Ahmed. Invertible generative models for inverse problems: mitigating representation error and dataset bias. In Proceedings of Machine Learning and Systems 2020, pages 4577–4587. 2020. [Online]
