Asymptotic Non-gaussianity of Generalized Likelihood Ratio Test

The Generalized Likelihood Ratio Test is designed to test hypothesis of the form $H_0 : y \sim p_0$ (simple null) and $H_1 : y \sim p_\theta$, $\theta \in \Theta \setminus \{\emptyset\}$. It is based on the quantity $$ L_n(Y^n) = \sup_{\theta \in \Theta}\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n \log \frac{P_\theta(y_i)}{P_0(y_i)} $$ where $Y^{n} = \begin{bmatrix} y_1 & y_2 & \ldots & y_n \end{bmatrix}$ is a vector of i.i.d. samples.

To compute $p$-values for this test and control the false alarm probability, we need to know the distribution of $L_n(Y^n)$ under $H_0$.

Example (Gaussian Means Testing). let $P_\theta = \Ncl(\theta, I_d)$. Then $$ \log \frac{P_\theta(y)}{P_0(y)} = -\frac{1}{2}\left( \|y-\theta\|_2^2 - \|y\|_2^2 \right) = \langle \theta, y\rangle - \frac{\|\theta\|_2^2}{2} $$ so the GLRT is $L_n(Y^n) = \sup_{\theta} \{\langle \theta, y \rangle - \frac{\|\theta\|^2_2}{2} \} = \frac{\|y\|^2}{2}$. Under the null hypotheses, $2 L_n(Y^n) \sim \chi^2(\nu=n)$.

It turns out that this is a general phenomenon!

Proposition (GLRT Asymptotics). assume that $\theta_0$ belongs to the interior and that $\theta \mapsto P_\theta$ satisfies the regularity conditions of maximum likelihood estimation. Then as $n \to \infty$, $$ -2 L_n(Y^n) \stackrel{d}{\longrightarrow}\chi^2(\nu=d) $$ where $d$ is the dimension of $\theta \in \Theta$.

Proof . The idea is that, since $\sup_{\theta \in \Theta} \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n \log P_\theta(y_i)$ is precisely the maximum likelihood, we can analyze GLRT using asymptotic normality. As $n \to \infty$, $\sqrt{n}(\hat{\theta}^{\mathsf{MLE}}_n - \theta_0) \stackrel{d}{\longrightarrow} \Ncl(0, I(\theta_0)^{-1})$, and the idea behind the proposition is that $$- 2n L_n(Y^n) \stackrel{d}{\longrightarrow} -2n \KL(P_{\theta_0} \mid P_{\theta^{\mathsf{MLE}}_n})$$ so that we can compute the limiting distribution using the Delta method. Set $G(\theta) = - \KL(P_{\theta_0} \mid P_{\theta})$, then $$ \begin{aligned} G(\theta_0) & = 0 \\ \nabla G (\theta_0) &= 0 \\ \nabla^2 G(\theta_0) & = \E_{y \sim \theta_0}\left[-\nabla^2 \log P_\theta(y)\right]\biggr \rvert_{\theta = \theta_0} = I(\theta_0) \end{aligned} $$ which implies that $$-2n L_n(Y^n) \stackrel{d}{\longrightarrow} \frac{n}{2} (\theta^{\mathsf{MLE}} - \theta_0)^T[\nabla^2 G(\theta_0)] (\theta^{\mathsf{MLE}} - \theta_0) \stackrel{d}{\longrightarrow} \chi^2(\nu=d).$$

This should generalize to any M-estimator! That is: if asymptotic normality holds for the parameter, then since the parameter is converging to a minimizer of the risk, the asymptotic distribution of the risk will be chi squared.
