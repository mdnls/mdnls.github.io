Dudley's Entropy Integral

Theorem (Dudley's Entropy Integral). let $(T, d)$ be a metric space and suppose that $(X_t)_{t \in T}$ is sub-gaussian process. Suppose further that the process is continuous in the sense that for any countable subset $T_0 \subseteq T$, $$ \lim_{T_0 \ni s \to t} X_s(\omega) = X_t(\omega) \qquad \text{for a.e. $\omega \in \Omega$} $$ Then, $$ \begin{aligned} \E\left[\sup_{t \in T} X_t\right] \leq 3 \sqrt{2}\, \sum_{k \in \Z} 2^{-k}\sqrt{\log \Ncl(T, d, 2^{-k})} \leq 6 \sqrt{2} \int_0^\infty \sqrt{\log \Ncl(T, d, \eps)} \, d\eps. \end{aligned} $$

Proof. let $T^k \subseteq T$ be a sequence of minimal $2^{-k}$ coverings of $T$. Let $\pi^k : T \to T^k$ be the projection onto $T^k$ and let $N_k = |T^k|$.

First, let $k_0 = \sup\{ k \in \Z : 2^{-k} > \diam(T)\}$. For all $k < k_0$, $N_k = 1$ and we may assume without loss of generality that $T^k = \{t_0\}$ for some point $t_0 \in T$. For any $t \in T$, we have almost surely that $$ X_t = X_{t_0} + \sum_{k=k_0+1}^\infty X_{\pi^k(t)} - X_{\pi^{k-1}(t)} $$ Where we used continuity of the process, along with the fact that $T^\infty \coloneqq \cup_{k \in \Z} T^k$ is a countable dense subset of $T$ and $t = \lim_{k \to \infty} \pi^k(t)$. Since $\E[X_{t_0}] = 0$, $$ \E\left[\sup_{t \in T} X_t \right] \leq \sum_{k=k_0+1}^\infty \E\left[ \sup_{t \in T} \{X_{\pi^k(t)} - X_{\pi^{k-1}(t)}\}\right] $$ Fix $k > k_0$. Each $X_{\pi^k(t)} - X_{\pi^{k-1}(t)}$ is sub-Gaussian with parameter $$d(\pi^k(t), \pi^{k-1}(t)) \leq d(\pi^k(t), t) + d(\pi^{k-1}, t) \leq 3 (2^{-k})$$The number of random variables is upper bound by the number of pairs $(\pi^k(t), \pi^{k-1}(t))$ which is at most $N_k N_{k-1} \leq N_k^2$. Invoke the Discrete Maximal Inequality: $$ \E\left[ \sup_{t \in T} X_t \right] \leq \sum_{k=k_0+1}^\infty 3(2^{-k}) \sqrt{2 \log (N_k^2)} \leq 3 \sqrt{2} \sum_{k\in \Z} 2^{-k} \sqrt{\log(N_k)} $$ and the first part of the claim follows.

The second part follows from the fact that the chaining sum can be viewed as a Riemann integral. The map $k \mapsto \log \Ncl(T, d, 2^{-k})$ is increasing in $k$ so $$ 2 \sum_{k \in \Z} 2^{-(k+1)} \sqrt{\log \Ncl(T, d, 2^{-k})} \leq 2 \int_0^\infty \sqrt{\log \Ncl(T, d, \eps)} \, d\eps. $$

Remark. It is possible to reduce the continuity assumption to one that $(X, T, d)$ is separable: there exists $T_0 \subset T$ such that $\lim_{T_0 \ni s \to t} X_s = X_t$ almost surely. Just apply the above bound to increasing finite subsets $\bar{T}_n \subseteq T_0$ and then invoke monotone convergence of $\E[\sup_{t \in \bar{T}^n} X_t] \nearrow \E[\sup_{t \in T} X_t]$.

In the presence of [[Lipschitz Stochastic Process|lipschitzness]], it is also possible to prove a truncated form of Dudley's bound. This form of the bound is useful when $\sqrt{\log \Ncl(T, d, \eps)}$ is not integrable as $\eps \to 0$. As an example, see the discussion of [[Parametric, Non-parametric, and Donsker Classes]].

**Theorem (truncated Dudley integral)**. Suppose that $(X_t)_{t \in T}$ is a [[Lipschitz Stochastic Process]] with (possibly random) Lipschitz constant $C$. Suppose further that $(X, T, d)$ satisfies the continuity property above. Then, $$ \E\left[ \sup_{t \in T} X_t \right] \leq \inf_{\eps > 0} \left\{ 2\eps \, \E[C] + 12 \int_\eps^\infty \sqrt{\log \Ncl(T, d, \eps)} \, d\eps \right\}$$

Proof. In the same setting as the proof above, let $n = \inf\{k \in \Z : 2^{-(k+1)} < \eps\}$. Then we can take $$ Xt = X{t0} + X{t} - X{\pi^n(t)} + \sum{k = k0 + 1}^{n} X{\pi^k(t)}-X_{\pi^{k-1}(t)} $$ and, by the same reasoning, we have $$ \begin{align*} \E \left[ \sup{t \in T}Xt\right] & \leq \E[\sup{t \in T} |Xt - X{\pi^n(t)}|] + 6 \sqrt{2} \sum{k=k_0+1}^n 2^{-(k+1)} \sqrt{\log \Ncl(T, d, 2^{-k})} \\ & \leq 2 \eps\,\E[C] + 6 \sqrt{2} \int_\eps^\infty \sqrt{\log \Ncl(T, d, \eps)} \, d\eps \end{align*} $$ since $d(t, \pi^n(t)) \leq 2^{-n} \leq 2 \eps$.

Finally, note that the proof techniques above can also be generalized to the situation when $(X_t)_{t \in T}$ is not a sub-gaussian process, but rather when its increments satisfy some heavier-tailed concentration inequality (such as sub-exponential or sub-wiebull tails).

**Theorem (generic dudley entropy integral)**. Suppose that $(X_t)_{t \in T}$ satisfies, $$ \log \E\left[ \exp \left( \frac{\lambda (Xt - Xs)}{d(t, s)}\right)\right] \leq \phi(\lambda) $$ for some convex function $\phi(\lambda)$ such that $\phi(0) = \phi'(0) = 0$. Then, $$ \E\left[ \sup{t\in T} Xt \right] \lesssim \int_0^\infty (\phi^*)^{-1}(2 \log \Ncl(T, d, \eps)) \, d \eps $$ and, if the process is also Lipschitz, then this integral can be truncated as in the previous theorem.

Proof. Apply the same proof techniques as above using the [[Discrete Maximal Inequality]].

Application to Empirical Processes

The one-step chaining bound can be applied in the following way to bound the supremum of an [[Empirical Process Theory|empirical process]]. Let $\Fcl$ be a function class, let $X_1, \ldots, X_n \sim \mu$ i.i.d., and let $G_n(f)$ be the empirical process, $$ Gn(f) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \sum{i=1}^n (f(Xi) - \E\mu[f(X)]). $$ By [[Hoeffding's Inequality]], $G_n(f) - G_n(g)$ is sub-gaussian with parameter $\|f-g\|_\infty^2/4$. Indeed, since $G_n(f) - G_n(g) = G_n(f-g)$, we have $$ \log \E\left[\exp(\lambda Gn(f-g))\right] = \sum{i=1}^n \log \E\left[\exp\left(\frac{\lambda}{\sqrt{n}}(f(Xi) - g(Xi))\right)\right] \leq \frac{\lambda^2\|f - g\|_\infty^2}{8} $$ As discussed in the [[One-step Chaining Inequality#Application to Empirical Processes|one-step chaining bound for empirical processes]], the process is also Lipschitz with constant $\sqrt{n}\sup_{f \in \Fcl}\|f\|_\infty$. Therefore the truncated Dudley integral reads, $$ \E \left[ \sup{f \in \Fcl} Gn(f)\right] \lesssim \inf{\eps \geq 0} \left\{ \eps \sqrt{n}\, \sup{f \in \Fcl}\|f\|\infty + \int\eps^\infty \sqrt{\log \Ncl(\Fcl, \|\cdot\|_\infty, \eps) }\, d\eps\right\}
