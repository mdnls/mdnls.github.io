Asymptotic Normality of MLE over Exponential Families

Theorem: for any minimal exponential family $\Pcl = \{p_\theta : \theta \in \Theta\}$ and for samples $y_1, \ldots, y_n \sim p_{\theta^*}$ i.i.d., the maximum likelihood estimator satisfies:

  1. The MLE $\hat{\theta}_n$ is consistent in probability $\hat{\theta}_n \stackrel{p}{\longrightarrow} \theta^*$.
  2. It is asymptotically normal with

$$ \sqrt{n}(\hat{\theta}_n - \theta^*) \stackrel{d}{\longrightarrow}\Ncl(0, (\nabla^2 \Phi(\theta^*))^{-1}) $$

Proof: We will use convexity properties of the partition function $\Phi(\theta)$ which are proven in the note on Exponential Families.

  1. The maximum likelihood estimator has the form,

$$ \hat{\theta}_n = \arg \sup_{\theta \in \R^m} \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n \left(\langle \phi(y_i), \theta\rangle - \Phi(\theta) \right) $$

  1. Under the assumption of minimality, $\Phi(\theta)$ is strongly convex, so the optimization is strongly concave. It has a unique maximizer $\hat{\theta}$ which satisfies the first order optimality condition,

$$ \nabla \Phi(\hat{\theta}_n) = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n \phi(y_i) = \hat{\mu}_n $$

  1. By strong convexity, $\nabla \Phi(\theta)$ is a differentiable invertible function. We may write $\hat{\theta}_n = (\nabla \Phi)^{-1}(\hat{\mu}_n)$. Consistency follows from the fact that $\hat{\mu}_n \stackrel{p}{\longrightarrow} \mu^* \coloneqq \nabla \Phi(\theta^*)$ by the law of large numbers.
  2. Asymptotic normality follows from the Delta Method,$$

\sqrt{n}(\hat{\theta}_n - \theta^) \stackrel{d}{\longrightarrow} \Ncl(0, (\nabla^2 \Phi(\theta^)^{-1}) \ \Sigma \ (\nabla^2 \Phi(\theta^*))^{-1}) $$where $\Sigma = \Cov[\sqrt{n}(\hat{\mu}_n - \mu^)] = \nabla^2 \Phi(\theta^)$.
