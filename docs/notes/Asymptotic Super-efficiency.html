Asymptotic Super-efficiency

Definition. let $p_\theta(y)$ a density and let $\hat{\theta}_n$ be an estimator for $\theta$. The estimator is said to be super-efficient if its efficiency matches the Cramér-Rao lower bound for all $\theta \in \Theta$, and improves on it for at least one $\theta \in \Theta$.

Example: Hodges' Estimator.

  • Let $p_\theta \in \{\Ncl(\theta, 1) : \theta \in \R\}$. Consider the estimator $$

T_n = \begin{cases} \hat{x}_n & |\hat{x}_n| \geq n^{-1/4} \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$ For any $\theta \not = 0$, with sufficiently large $n \gg 1$, it follows from the CLT that $P(|\hat{x}_n - \theta| \leq n^{-1/4}) \to 0$ in probability and hence $\sqrt{n}(T_n - \theta) \stackrel{d}{\longrightarrow} \Ncl(0, 1)$. But when $\theta = 0$, by a similar argument, $P(|\hat{x}_n - \theta| \leq n^{-1/4}) \to 1$ which converges faster than what is predicted by the CLT.

  • This example is a trick! It abuses the fact that asymptotic efficiency is a pointwise diagnostic for each $\theta \in \Theta$. While enjoying excellent performance at $\theta = 0$, it has extremely poor performance in a shrinking neighborhood of zero.

Hàjek-Le Cam Convolution Theorem: consider an estimator $\theta_n$ such that for some family of R.V.s $\{V_\theta : \theta \in \Theta\}$, $\sqrt{n}(\theta_n - \theta) \stackrel{d}{\longrightarrow} V_\theta$ for every $\theta$. Then there is a family of R.V.s $\{U_\theta : \theta \in \Theta\}$ such that, $$ V_\theta \stackrel{d}{=} Z_\theta + U_\theta \qquad \text{a.e. $\theta \in \Theta$} $$ where $Z_\theta \sim N(0, [I(\theta)]^{-1})$ is independent of $U_\theta$.

Remark: as a consequence of independence, $\Cov_\theta(V_\theta) = \Cov(Z_\theta) + \Cov(U_\theta) \succcurlyeq [I(\theta)]^{-1}$. So, the Cramér-Rao bound holds for almost every $\theta \in \Theta$.
