Likelihood Ratio Test

Simple Likelihood Ratio Test

Definition: consider a hypothesis test for which the null is $H_0 : y_i \sim P_0$ and the alternative is $H_1 : y_i \sim P_1$, where in either case samples are i.i.d. for $i = 1 \ldots n$. The Likelihood Ratio Statistic is, $$ L_n(Y^n_1) = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n \log \frac{P_1(y_i)}{P_0(y_i)} $$ where $Y_1^n = \begin{bmatrix} y_1 & y_2 & \ldots & y_n \end{bmatrix}$, and a Likelihood Ratio Test takes the form $$ \Psi_t(Y^n_1) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if $L_n(Y^n_1) \geq t$} \\ 0 & \text{else} \end{cases}. $$ when $n=1$, it is common to omit the logarithm and to consider the test $$ \Psi_t(y) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if $\frac{p_1(y)}{p_0(y)} $ > t } \\ 0 & \text{else} \end{cases}. $$

Intuition: the key property of the LRS is that $$ \begin{aligned} \E_{P_1}[L_n(Y^n_1)] & = \KL(P_1 |P_0) \qquad \E_{P_0}[L_n(Y^n_1)] = -\KL(P_0 | P_1). \end{aligned} $$ So, the threshold estimator is designed to test for cases when $\KL(P_1 | P_0)$ is large.

Generalized Likelihood Ratio Test

Definition: a composite hypothesis has the form $H_{\Theta} : \{y_i \sim p_\theta \text{ for some } \theta \in \Theta\}$. A composite test is one in which either the null or the alternative are composite hypotheses.

In the case $H_0 : y \sim p_0$ (simple null) and $H_1 : y \sim p_\theta$, $\theta \in \Theta \setminus \{\emptyset\}$, the Composite Likelihood Ratio Statistic is $$ L_n(Y^n_1) = \sup_{\theta \in \Theta}\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n \log \frac{P_\theta(y_i)}{P_0(y_i)}. $$ and the Generalized Likelihood Ratio Test (GLRT) takes the form $$ \Psi_t(Y^n_1) = \begin{cases} 1 & L_n(Y^n_1) > t \\ 0 & \mathsf{else} \end{cases} $$
