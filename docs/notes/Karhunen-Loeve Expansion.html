Karhunen-Loeve Expansion

The Karhunen-Loeve expansion is a representation for stochastic processes. Let $X(s, \omega) : \Dcl \times \Omega \to \R$ with with covariance kernel $C(s, t) \coloneqq \Cov(X(s, \omega), X(t, \omega))$. Let $\{(\lambda_i, \phi_i)\}_{i\geq 1}$ be the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the operator $\Lambda : \Dcl \to \Dcl$ defined by, $$ \Lambda f(s) = \int_{t \in \Dcl} C(s, t) f(t) \, dt.$$ Definition. Karhunen-Loeve Expansion. Define $Z_i(s, \omega)$ by, $$ Z_i(\omega) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\lambda_i}} \int_{\Dcl} (X(s, \omega) - \mu(s)) \phi_i(s) \, ds. $$ Then $X(s, \omega)$ can be represented as, $$ X(s, \omega) = \mu(s) + \sum_{i\geq 1} \sqrt{\lambda_i} \phi_i(s) Z_i(\omega). $$

Properties and Remarks:

  • The idea behind this process is to make use of the fact that for any orthonormal basis of $\Lcl^2(\Dcl)$, the process $X(s, \omega)$ admits a decomposition with random coefficients in the same form as Karhunen-Loeve.
  • Decorrelation Property: the reason why eigenfunctions of $C(s, t)$ are a smart choice is because:

$$\begin{aligned} \E[Z_i(\omega) Z_j(\omega)] & = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\lambda_i \lambda_j}}\E\left[\int_\Dcl(X(s, \omega) - \mu(s) )(X(t, \omega) - \mu(t) ) \phi_i(s) \phi_j(t)\, ds \, dt \right] \\ & = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\lambda_i \lambda_j}} \int_{\Dcl} C(s, t) \phi_i(s) \phi_j(t)\, ds \, dt \\ & = 1\{i = j\}. \end{aligned}$$ So, the $\{Z_i(\omega)\}_{i \geq 1}$ are uncorrelated.

  • Essentially, we are using a basis of $\Lcl^2(\Dcl)$ to construct a set of orthonormal random variables $Z_i(\omega)$ under the inner product $\langle Z_i, Z_j\rangle = \E[Z_i Z_j]$.

Accuracy of Approximation: the Karhunen-Loeve expansion satisfies a version of the Eckert-Young Theorem. Let $X_r = \sum_{i \leq r} \sqrt{\lambda_i} \phi_i(s) Z_i(\omega)$. Let $S^r = \{ X'(s, \omega) : \exists K_r \text{ an }r\text{ dim subspace of }\Lcl^2(\Dcl), X'(s, \omega) \in K_r,\ \omega \text{-a.s.} \}$. Then, $$ \E[\|X - X_r \|_{\Lcl^2(\Dcl)}^2] = \inf_{Y_r \in S^r} \E[\|X - Y_r\|_{\Lcl^2(\Dcl)}^2]. $$
