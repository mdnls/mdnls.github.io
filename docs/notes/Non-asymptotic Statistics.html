    • Methods tailored specifically to empirical processes
      • Chaining Inequalities
        • One-step chaining for empirical processes
        • Dudley Entropy for empirical processes
        • The moral of the story is that both of the above bounds require sub-gaussianity of the empirical process. The sub-gaussian parameter is essentially controlled by $\sup_{f \in \Fcl} \|f\|_{\infty}$ through Hoeffding's bound, leading to coverings in $\|\cdot \|_\infty$. For many function classes of interest, such as indicator functions and lipschitz functions, the $\log \Ncl(\Fcl, \|\cdot\|_\infty, \eps)$ grows too fast in $\eps^{-1}$ to prove useful bounds.

        • Ossiander's Theorem – a Dudley-type bound for $\Ncl_{[]}(\Fcl, \|\cdot\|_{\Lcl^2(\mu)}, \eps)$ that can be used to prove the optimal Glivenko-Cantelli bound with a bracketing entropy.
