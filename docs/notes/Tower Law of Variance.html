Tower Law of Variance

Tower Law of Variance: let $(X, Y)$ be random variables with joint distribution $\pi$. The marginal variance $\Var_y(Y)$ can be decomposed as, $$ \Vary(Y) = \Ex[\Var(Y \mid X)] + \Var_x(\E[Y \mid X]). $$ Here, the notation $\E_x[ \cdot ]$ and $\Var_x(\cdot)$ represent the variance of a function of $X\sim \pi$ _marginalized_ over $Y$, and likewise for $\E_y[\cdot]$ and $\Var_y(\cdot)$.

_Proof_: by direct evaluation, $$ \begin{align*} \Ey[(Y - \Ey[Y])^2] & = \E[(Y - \E[Y\mid X] + \E[Y \mid X] - \E_y[Y])^2] \\ & = \underbrace{\E[(Y - \E[Y \mid X])^2]}{(1)} + \underbrace{\E[(\E[Y \mid X] - \Ey[Y])^2]}{(2)} + 2 \underbrace{\E[(Y - \E[Y \mid X])(\E[Y \mid X] - \Ey[Y])]}_{(3)} \end{align*} $$ These terms are, $$ \begin{align*} (1) & = \E_x[\Var(Y \mid X)] \\ (2) & = \Var_x[\E[Y \mid X]] \\ (3) & = \Ex [\E[ Y - \E[Y \mid X] \, \mid X] (\E[Y \mid X] - \Ey[Y])\\ & = (\E[Y \mid X] - \E[Y \mid X])(\E[Y \mid X] - \E_y[Y]) \\ & = 0 \end{align*}
