Exponential Families

Description: an exponential family is a parametrized family of distributions $\{p_\theta : \theta \in \Theta\}$ which have the form $$ p_\theta(y) = h(y) \exp\left(\langle \phi(y), \theta \rangle - \Phi(\theta)\right). $$ The vector $\phi : \Ycl \to \R^m$ contains the Sufficient Statistics for $y$. The vector $\theta \in \R^m$ is known as the natural parameters of the family. The log normalization $\Phi(\theta)$ is closely related to the Cumulant Generating Function, and it is also known as the partition function.

Minimal Exponential Family: an exponential family is minimal if there does not exist $a \in \R^m \setminus \{0\}$ such that $$ \langle \phi(y), a \rangle = 0 \qquad \text{for $h(y) dy$-almost every $y$.} $$ This quantity can be thought of as a requirement that the components of $\phi(y)$ are linearly independent in $\Lcl^2(h(y) \, dy)$.

Moment vs. Parameter Duality: the CGF maps the natural parameters to moments of the sufficient statistics: $$ \begin{aligned} \nabla_\theta \Phi(\theta) & = \frac{\int \phi(y) \exp(\langle \phi(y), \theta \rangle )h(y) \, dy }{\int \exp(\langle \phi(y), \theta \rangle) h(y) \, dy} = \E_\theta[\phi(y)] \\ \nabla_\theta^2 \Phi(\theta)& = \Cov_\theta[\phi(y)] \end{aligned} $$ the convex conjugate of $\Phi(\theta)$ is an entropy type quantity $\Psi(\mu)$ given by, $$\begin{aligned} \Psi(\mu) & = \sup_{\theta \in \R^m} \langle \theta, \mu \rangle - \Phi(\theta) \end{aligned} $$ By standard properties of the convex conjugate, $\nabla_\mu \Psi(\mu) = \theta^*(\mu)$ where $\theta^*(\mu) = \arg\,\sup_{\theta \in \R^m} \langle \theta, \mu \rangle - \Phi(\theta)$. The first order optimality condition is that $\theta^*(\mu) = \nabla_\theta \Phi(\theta^*) = \E_{\theta^*}[\phi(y)]$. The duality property is,

>> The partition function $\Phi(\theta)$ is (convex) dual to the entropy $\Psi(\mu)$. There is a canonical pairing $(\theta, \mu) \in \Mcl$ between parameters and moments of statistics, such that each parameter $\theta \in \R^m$ has $(\theta, \nabla_\theta \Phi(\theta)) \in \Mcl$ and each moment $\mu \in \R^m$ has $(\nabla_\mu \Psi(\mu), \mu) \in \Mcl$.

These canonical pairings also arise in statistical mechanics in the form of thermodynamical relations. (However, they're usually explainbed in a non-rigorous and confusing way. See: Maxwell Relations, Thermodynamic Square)

Theorem: if $\{p_\theta : \theta \in \Theta\}$ is a minimal exponential family, then $\nabla_\theta \Phi(\theta)$ and $\nabla_\mu \Psi(\mu)$ are invertible and differentiable, thus hence the canonical pairing between moments and parameters is unique.

Proof: the Hessian is $\nabla^2_\theta \Phi(\theta) = \Cov_\theta[\phi(y)]$. Under the assumption of minimality, $\Var_\theta[\langle a, \phi(y)\rangle] = a^T \Cov_\theta[\phi(0)] a > 0$ for $a \not = 0$ since if this variance can only be zero if $\langle a, \phi(y) \rangle = 0$ for $h(y)dy$-almost every $y$. It follows from properties of Convex Conjugate that $(\nabla_\theta \Phi, \nabla_\mu \Psi)$ are an invertible pair.
