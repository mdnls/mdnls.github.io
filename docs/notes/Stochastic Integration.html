Stochastic Integration

It is possible to define a notion of integration with respect to continuous local martingales and, more generally, continuous semi-martingales. The simplest possible situation is when we would like to integrate a progressive process of the form $$ H = \sum_{i=1}^p Z_{i} \, 1 \{ t_{i-1} < t \leq t_i\} \qquad Z_i \text{ is bounded, $\Fcl_{t_i}$-measurable}. $$ in which case the stochastic integral is given by $$ (H \cdot X)_t = \sum_{i=1}^p Z_i(M_{t_i} - M_{t_{i-1}}) + \sum_{i=1}^p Z_i (V_{t_i} - V_{t_{i+1}}). $$ More generally, for any progressive process $H$ (satisfying some boundedness assumptions), $$ (H \cdot X)_t = \int_0^t H_s \, d M_s + \int_0^t H_s\, dV_s $$ where the former integral is the stochastic integral of a continuous local martingale and the latter is the integral of a finite variation process.

Itô's Formula

Theorem (Itô's Formula). let $X^1, \ldots, X^k$ be $k$ continuous semi-martingales and let $F$ be a twice continuous differentiable function on $\R^p$. Then for each $t \geq 0$ we have $$ \begin{aligned} F(X_t^1,\ldots, X_t^k) & = F(X_0^1, \ldots, X_0^k) + \sum_{i=1}^p \int_0^t \partial_{x_i} F (X_s^1, \ldots, X_s^k) \, d X_s \\ & \quad +\, \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i,j=1}^p \int_0^t \partial_{x_i x_j} F(X_s^1,\ldots,X_s^k) \, d \langle X^i, X^j \rangle_s \end{aligned} $$

Proof sketch. use Taylor's formula on a grid and take the limit as grid mesh size goes to zero.

Moment Formulas

It turns out that the bracket commutes with stochastic integration:

Proposition (brackets commute with stochastic integral). Let $H \in \Lcl^2(M)$ and $M, N \in \H$. Then, if $K \in \Lcl^2(N)$, $$ \langle H \cdot M, K \cdot N \rangle = HK \cdot \langle M, N\rangle $$ Taking (for example) $N = H \cdot M$, it turns out that $K \in \Lcl^2( H \cdot M)$ iff $HK \in \Lcl^2(M)$.

Finally, by taking expectations of the above identity: $$ \E\left[ \int_0^t H_s \, dM_s \right] = 0 \qquad \qquad \E\left[\left( \int_0^t H_s \, d M_s \right) \left( \int_0^t K_s \, d N_s \right)\right] = \E\left[\int_0^t H_s K_s \, d \langle M , N \rangle_s \right] $$
