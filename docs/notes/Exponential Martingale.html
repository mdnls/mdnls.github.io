Exponential Martingale

Suppose that $X$ is a continuous local martingale. The exponential martingale $\Ecl(\lambda X)$, where $\lambda \in \C$, is a continuous local martingale given by $$ \Ecl(\lambda X)_t = \exp\left( \lambda X_t - \frac{\lambda^2}{2} \langle X\rangle_t\right). $$

The fact that $\Ecl(\lambda X)$ is a continuous local martingale can be seen from Ito's formula. Indeed, viewing $\Ecl(\lambda X)_t = F(\lambda X_t, \lambda^2 \langle X \rangle_t)$ for $F(x, y) = e^{x - y/2}$ yields $$ \begin{aligned} \Ecl(\lambda X)_t & = e^{\lambda X_0} + \lambda \int_0^t \Ecl(\lambda X)_s \, d X_s - \frac{\lambda^2}{2} \int_0^t \Ecl(\lambda X)_s \, d \langle X \rangle_s + \frac{\lambda^2}{2} \int _0^t \Ecl(\lambda X)_s \, d \langle X \rangle_s \\ & = e^{\lambda X_0} + \lambda\int_0^t \Ecl(\lambda X)_s \, d X_s \end{aligned} $$ Here, we used the fact that $\langle X, \langle X \rangle \rangle = \langle \langle X \rangle \rangle = 0$ because $\langle X \rangle$ is a finite variation function.

Proposition (representation by exponential martingales). Suppose that $M_t$ is a continuous local martingale and that it is strictly positive in that $\inf_{t \geq 0}M_t > 0$ almost surely. Then there exists a continuous local martingale $L$ such that $M_t = \Ecl(L)_t$.

Proof. since $M$ is continuous, we may define a stopping time $T_\eps = \inf \{t \geq 0 : M_t \leq \eps\}$ (cf. entry times). Then $M^{T_\eps}$ is a continuous local martingale with $\inf_{t \geq 0} M^{T_\eps}_t \geq \eps$, so we may apply ItÃ´'s formula to $\log M^{T_\eps}_t$: $$ \log M^{T_\eps}_t = \log M^{T_\eps}_0 + \int_0^t \frac{d M^{T_\eps}_s}{M_s^{T_\eps}} - \frac{1}{2} \int_0^t \frac{d \langle M^{T_\eps} \rangle_s}{(M_s^{T_\eps})^2} $$ So, we can choose $$L^{\eps}_t = \log M_0^{T_\eps} + \int_0^t \frac{d M_s^{T_\eps}}{M_s^{T_\eps}} \qquad \implies \qquad\langle L^{\eps} \rangle_t = \int_0^t \frac{d \langle M^{T_\eps}\rangle_s}{(M^{T_\eps}_s)^2} $$ and it has $M_t^{T_\eps} = \Ecl(L^\eps)_t$. Finally, since it is true almost surely that $\inf_{t \geq 0} M_t > 0$, it also holds a.s. that $$ L_t \coloneqq \log M_0^T + \int_0^t \frac{d M_s}{M_s} \qquad \implies \qquad M_t = \Ecl(L)_t. $$

Uniform Integrability

In many applications (notably when using Girsanov's Theorem) it is valuable to be able to show that $\Ecl(L)$ is uniformly integrable. The following theorem states some conditions when this is the case.

Theorem (conditions for u.i. density ratios). Let $L$ be a continuous local martingale with $L_0 = 0$. Consider the following properties:

  1. Novikov's criterion: $\E\left[\exp \left(\frac{1}{2} \langle L \rangle_\infty\right)\right] < \infty$.
  2. Kazamaki's criterion: $L$ is a u.i. martingale with $\E\left[ \exp\left( \frac{1}{2} L_\infty \right)\right] < \infty$.
  3. $\Ecl(L)$ is a uniformly integrable martingale.
