Modes of probabilistic convergence

Convergence of probability measures: Let $(\mu_n)_{n \geq 1}$ be a sequence of measures on $(\Omega, \mathcal{F})$ and let $f : \Omega \to \mathbb{R}$ a generic function. Assume that $\Omega$ is a Polish space to avoid pathological issues such as failure of the Portmanteau Lemma (?).

  1. In TV: $\text{TV}(\mu_n, \mu_\infty) \to 0$ where $\text{TV}$ is total variation distance:

$$ \text{TV}(\mu_n, \mu_\infty) = \sup_{f \text{ msbrl}} | \mu_n(f) - \mu_\infty(f) |$$

  1. In Wasserstein-1: convergence with respect to the metric,

$$W_1(\mu_n, \mu_\infty) = \sup_{f \text{ 1-lip}} |\mu_n(f) - \mu_\infty(f)|$$

  1. Strong (Setwise) convergence: for every $A \in \mathcal{F}$, $\mu_n(A) \to \mu_\infty(A)$.
  2. Weak convergence: for every bounded continuous $f$, $\mathbb{E}_{\mu_n}[f] \to \mathbb{E}_{\mu_\infty}[f]$. cf Portmanteau Lemma.

Convergence of random variables: Let $(X_n)_{n \geq 1}$ be a sequence of real-valued random variables on the measure space $(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mathbb{P})$.

  1. Pointwise: for all $\omega \in \mathcal{F}$, $X_n(\omega) \to X_\infty(\omega)$.
  2. Almost sure: for $\mathbb{P}$-almost every $\omega \in \Omega$, $X_n(\omega) \to X_\infty(\omega)$.
  3. In probability: for each $\eps$, $\lim_{n \to \infty} \mathbb{P}(| X_n(\omega) - X_\infty(\omega)|>\eps) = 0$.
  4. In $\mathcal{L}^p$: $\lim_{n \to \infty} \mathbb{E}[|X_n - X_\infty|^p] = 0$.
  5. In distribution: $\lim_{n \to \infty} \mathbb{P}(X_n \leq x) = F_{X_\infty}(x)$ at each $x$ where $F_{X_\infty}$ is continuous. That is, pointwise convergence of the CDF where its limit is continuous.

  • Continuous Mapping Theorem
