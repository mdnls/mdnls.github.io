Packing and Covering Numbers

Packing and covering numbers can be used to describe the complexity of a subset $S$ of a generic metric space. Intuitively, packing numbers are the maximum number of $\eps$-separated points that can be contained in $S$, whereas covering numbers are the minimum number of points required to cover $S$ with $\eps$-balls. In the former case, one seeks to pack in as many disjoint $\eps$-balls as possible, whereas in the latter case the goal is to cover the set with very few $\eps$-balls.

Definition (packing and covering numbers). Let $(\Xcl, d)$ be a metric space, let $S \subseteq \Xcl$, and let $B(x, \eps)$ correspond to the $\eps$-ball around $x \in \Xcl$.

  1. Packing number: $\Pcl(S, d, \eps)$ is the maximum cardinality of a subset $P \subseteq S$ such that $\forall \, x,y \in P$, $d(x, y) \geq \eps$. In other words, $B(x, \eps/2) \cap B(y, \eps/2) = \emptyset$. A packing is sometimes also called an $\eps$-separated set.
  2. Covering number: $\Ncl(S, d, \eps)$ is the minimum cardinality of a subset $P \subset S$ such that $\forall\, x \in S$, $$

\min_{y \in P} d(x, y) < \eps $$

Examples

  • Packing the unit cube in $\| \cdot \|_\infty$ metric: $\Pcl([0, 1]^d, \|\cdot\|_\infty, \eps) = (\lfloor \frac{1}{\eps}\rfloor )^d$ for $\eps < 1$. The packing is just a bunch of $\eps$-cubes packed inside the $[0, 1]^d$ cube.
  • Packing norm balls inside $\R^d$: suppose $\| \cdot \|$ satisfies $\|cx \| = c \|x\|$ for $c \in \R_+$ and $x \in \R^d$. Consider $S = B(0, r) = \{x \in \R^d : \|x\|\leq r\}$. Then we have $$ \left( \frac{r}{\eps} \right)^d \leq \Ncl(S, \| \cdot \|, \eps) \leq \left(1 + \frac{2r}{\eps} \right)^d.$$It is a consequence of the property, $$ \mathsf{Vol}(B(0,\eps)) = \mathsf{Vol}(\eps B(0, 1)) = \eps^d \,\mathsf{Vol}(B(0, 1))$$and so, for any $\eps$-packing $P$, $$ (r+\eps/2)^d \, \mathsf{Vol}(B(0, 1)) = \mathsf{Vol}(B(0, r+\eps/2)) \geq \sum_{x \in P} \mathsf{Vol}(B(x, \eps/2)) = |P|\, (\eps/2)^d \, \mathsf{Vol}(B(0, 1)) $$which implies that $|P| \leq \left( \frac{2r}{\eps} + 1\right)^d$. The lower bound follows by a similar argument, using a union bound to control $\mathsf{Vol}(B(0, r)) \leq |P| \, \mathsf{Vol}(B(0, \eps))$ where $P$ is a $\eps$-covering. A common form bound appears with $r =1$, $\|\cdot\| = \|\cdot\|_2$, and when $\eps < 1$ it implies $(\frac{1}{\eps})^d \leq |P| \leq (\frac{3}{\eps})^d$.

Equivalence between packing and covering

Proposition (packing and covering equivalence). Let $S \subseteq \Xcl$, then $$ \Pcl(S, d, 2\eps) \leq \Ncl(S, d, \eps) \leq \Pcl(S, d, \eps) $$

Proof. For the first inequality, some good intuition is that any $2\eps$-packing $P$ can be viewed as a collection of disjoint $\eps$-balls contained in $S$, which can be converted to an $\eps$-covering by adding more $\eps$-balls to $P$. However, this is not a full proof, since we need to show that the maximal packing is upper bound in size by a minimal covering. Let $P$ be a maximal $\eps$-packing and let $Q$ be minimal $\eps$-covering respectively.

Take $\pi(x) = \arg \inf_{y \in Q} d(x, y)$ the projection onto $Q$. Then, for $x, x' \in P$, the triangle inequality implies that $\pi(x) = \pi(x') \implies d(x, x') \leq d(x, \pi(x)) + d(x', \pi(x)) = 2\eps$. The fact that $P$ is a $2\eps$-packing forces $x=x'$. Thus $\pi$ is a one-to-one mapping and $|Q| \geq |\pi(P)| = |P|$.

For the second inequality, note that if $P$ is a maximal $\eps$-packing, then for any $x \in S$, it is impossible that $\inf_{y \in P} d(x, y) > \eps$ since otherwise $\{x\} \cup P$ would also be an $\eps$-packing. The fact that $P$ is also an $\eps$-covering forces $|P| \geq \Ncl(S, d, \eps)$.
