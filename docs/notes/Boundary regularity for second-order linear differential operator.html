Boundary regularity for second-order linear differential operator

Generally speaking, the role of the uniform ellipticity condition is in bounding the highest possible derivative of a solution, in terms of some energy functional associated with the operator. Then, one uses case-by-case methods (e.g. many applications of Cauchy-Schwartz) to handle lower order terms and coerce everything into a bound on a Sobolev norm of the solution, in terms of lower order data of the solution and/or PDE coefficients.
