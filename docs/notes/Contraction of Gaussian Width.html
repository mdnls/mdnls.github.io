Contraction of Gaussian Width

Proposition (lipschitz contraction of Gaussian width. let $\{\phi_i\}_{i=1}^d$ be a collection of $1$-Lipschitz functions, and let $\Theta \subseteq \R^d$. Then, for $w_1, \ldots, w_d \sim \Ncl(0, 1)$ i.i.d., $$ \Gcl(\phi(\Theta)) = \E \left[ \sup_{\theta \in \Theta} \sum_{i=1}^d \phi_i(\theta_i) w_i\right] \leq \E\left[ \sup_{\theta \in \Theta} \sum_{i=1}^d \theta_i w_i \right]=\Gcl(\Theta) $$

Proof. Apply Sudakov-Fernique Inequality to the process $X_\theta = \sum_{i=1}^d\phi_i(\theta_i)w_i$ and $Y_\theta = \sum_{i=1}^d \theta_i w_i$. Then, $$ \E[|X_{\theta_1} - X_{\theta_2}|^2] = \sum_{i=1}^d (\phi_i(\theta_{1i}) - \phi_i(\theta_{2i}))^2 \leq \sum_{i=1}^d (\theta_{1i}-\theta_{2i})^2 = \E[|Y_{\theta_1}-Y_{\theta_2}|^2] $$ and the proof follows by taking the limit of discretizations of $\Theta$.
