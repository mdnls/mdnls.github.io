Slutsky's Theorem

Theorem: Let $X_1, X_2, \ldots$ and $Y_1, Y_2, \ldots$ be two sequences of random variables such that $X_n \stackrel{i.d.}{\longrightarrow}X_\infty$ and $Y_n \stackrel{p}{\longrightarrow} c$ where $c$ is a constant. Then for any continuous function $g(x, y)$, $g(X_n, Y_n) \stackrel{i.d.}{\longrightarrow} g(X_\infty, c)$.

This theorem lets you prove a lot of properties about convergence of sums/products/etc of random variables.
