Publications

Invertible generative models for inverse problems: mitigating representation error and dataset bias.

In an imaging inverse problem, one must recover missing information about a target image by leveraging an assumed prior distribution from which the target was sampled. We show that Invertible Neural Networks, when trained as a generative model of a class of natural images, can provide a strong image prior in a variety of inverse problems. Published in ICML 2020.


M. Asim*, M. Daniels*, O. Leong, P. Hand, and A. Ahmed. Invertible generative models for inverse problems: mitigating representation error and dataset bias. In Proceedings of Machine Learning and Systems 2020, pages 4577–4587. 2020. [Online]

Statistical Distances and Their Implications to GAN Training.

This is an interactive article about the role of statistical distances like Kullback Leibler Divergence and Earth Mover's Distance in training Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). Honorable mention for best submission to the VISxAI workshop at IEEE VIS 2019.


M. Daniels. Statistical Distances and their Implications to GAN Training. In the VISxAI Workshop at IEEE VIS 2019. [Online]
